Cuisine - the Jain Way

Jain Food Philosophy

Jainism has contributed tremendously to the cause of compassion and nonviolence in the world today. Jains consider nonviolence as one of their most essential religious duties. For Jains, the practice of applying nonviolence in their daily activities begins with limiting violence in their diet. Jains do not use or consume products obtained from dead animals; they are either lacto-vegetarians or vegans. Being a vegetarian is just not enough; efforts are made to avoid unnecessary injury to plants and other minuscule organisms. The Jain diet does not consist of root vegetables such as potatoes, onions, garlic and tubers, because such root vegetables, although are considered one article, have countless lives (known as ananthkay in Sanskrit). Moreover, consumption of most root vegetables involves uprooting and eventually killing the entire plant. The practice of mindful eating with careful selection of ingredients and preparation of food thus reinforces the principles of nonviolence and provides spiritual benefits.

Items not allowed in Jain Recipes

  • Root vegetables (carrot, potato, beat,ginger, etc..)
  • Spinach
  • Ginger
  • Mushroom
  • Egg plant
  • Honey
  • Figs
  • Gelatin
  • Potato starch
  • Tapioca starch
  • Tapioca (Sabudana)
  • Butter
  • Cheese with animal rennet, potato starch, animal enzyme
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Vinegar
  • Yeast

Items to be avoided during Tithi Observance Days:

  • Any Fruits - Fresh and Dried
  • Any Vegetables including Peppers, Lemons and Tomatoes
  • Raw Bananas
  • Butter
  • Frozen Foods (including vegetables and coconut)
  • Cashew nuts
  • Dry Herbs (Methi,Mint,Curry Leaves,Oregano,Parsley,Green Tea etc)
  • Bean Sprouts, Parsley,Green
  • Vinegar
  • Coconut Milk
  • Sour Cream
  • Ready Made Bread (Store brands, Pita, Pizza bread, Nan, Roti, Parathas)
  • Canned Food
  • Frozen Meals or Vacuum Packed Meals, Instant Mixes
  • Yeast
